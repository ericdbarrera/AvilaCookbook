Smoked Brisket

Serving size: 10-12

Recipe by: Johnny Montemayor Sr.

Ingredients:
  • 10-12 lbs Brisket
  • 20 lbs regular Charcoal - preferable Mesquite
  • Brisket Rub
  • Garlic Powder
  • Salt
Instructions:
  1. Start fire and make sure the charcoals are glowing red.
  2. Place brisket on the grill to sear on both sides. Brisket will flame up and burn the outer edges lightly. This seals in the juices and makes the brisket tender.
  3. Once seared remove and season all sides with brisket rub & garlic powder
  4. Double wrap brisket in heavy-duty aluminum foil. Make sure the brisket is sealed.
  5. Place back on grill over the fire for about 2 hours. Flip over for another 45 minutes to an hour. Remove from grill or cook for longer. its a brisket.
  6. Save juices from brisket, it will be used for bbq sauce later.
  7. Let brisket cool down completely, then place in refrigerator overnight. This will make it easier to slice.
BBQ Sauce Instructions:
  1. Once cooled, place juices in refrigerator.
  2. Once the fat from juices has hardened, remove from liquid. Should have gelatin consistency
  3. Mix 50% gelatin & 50% BBQ sauce. Heat in a sauce pan and pour over brikset when warming up to serve.
